Getting Around Denver

RTD Light Rail

The A Line runs from DEN airport to Union Station ($10.50). From Union Station, the C/E lines reach Ball Arena, 16th Street Mall, and LoDo. Day pass covers unlimited rides.

Airport to downtown: $10.50

Free 16th Street Mall Shuttle

The MallRide is a free electric bus running the length of 16th Street through downtown. Connects Union Station to Civic Center. Great for restaurant hopping.

Cost: FREE

Rideshare

Uber/Lyft work well. Airport to downtown runs $30-40. Post-game surge is brutal - walk 10 mins away from venues before requesting.

Walking

Coors Field and Ball Arena are both walkable from downtown hotels. LoDo (Lower Downtown) is the bar/restaurant district adjacent to Coors Field. Plan accordingly.

Where to Stay

The Altitude Factor

Real Talk: 5,280 Feet Matters

Denver sits one mile above sea level. If you're visiting from sea level, you'll feel it. Drink extra water, take it easy on the alcohol (it hits harder), and don't be surprised if you're winded walking uphill. This is also why Coors Field is famous for home runs - the thin air carries balls further.

The Beer Warning

Denver has 150+ craft breweries. You will drink at altitude. You will get drunk faster. Pace yourself or pay the price. The locals know - they'll be two drinks ahead of you and still standing.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the cheapest sport to watch in Denver?

Colorado Rockies baseball. The Rockpile section offers $8 day-of tickets for center field bleacher seats. Rooftop standing room runs $15-25. Coors Field is one of America's best ballpark values with mountain views included at no extra charge.

How do I get from Denver airport to downtown?

RTD A Line light rail runs from DEN to Union Station (downtown) for $10.50 one-way. Takes about 37 minutes. Trains run every 15 minutes. From Union Station, you can walk to LoDo/Coors Field or transfer to the C/E lines for Ball Arena. Much cheaper than the $35-45 Uber.

Can I see both Nuggets and Avalanche in one trip?

Yes - both teams play at Ball Arena. During winter months (November-March), back-to-back or same-weekend games are common. Check both schedules before booking. Two championship-caliber teams, one arena, one weekend trip.

Does altitude really affect the experience?

Yes. At 5,280 feet, you'll notice faster intoxication (drink water between beers), potential breathlessness when walking uphill, and possibly headaches if you're sensitive. Stay hydrated and pace yourself. The thin air also makes Coors Field a home run paradise - balls carry 9% further than at sea level.

The Mile High Experience

Two recent champions. One of baseball's most beautiful parks. Light rail that actually works. Denver is an underrated sports city that delivers championship-level experiences without championship-level prices.
