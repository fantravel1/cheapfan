The Arlington Reality

Arlington (Cowboys + Rangers) has no public transit by choice. They voted it down. You need a car or rideshare. Parking at AT&T Stadium runs $50-100. This hidden cost makes Cowboys games even more expensive. For Mavs and Stars, DART light rail from anywhere in Dallas works perfectly.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the cheapest sport in Dallas?

Dallas Stars hockey and FC Dallas soccer offer the best value. Stars weeknight games start at $25, and FC Dallas tickets begin at $18. Both are playoff-caliber teams in markets where football and basketball dominate attention, keeping prices lower. Rangers baseball offers $18 upper deck in an air-conditioned stadium.

Can I use public transit for Dallas sports?

For Mavericks and Stars at American Airlines Center, yes - DART light rail stops nearby ($2.50). For Cowboys and Rangers in Arlington, no - Arlington has no public transit. You'll need a car or rideshare. Parking at AT&T Stadium runs $50-100. This geography gap is the biggest challenge for budget sports travel in DFW.

Is the Cowboys Party Pass worth it?

Yes - especially for first-time visitors. The Party Pass ($95-125) gives standing-room access to AT&T Stadium's end zone with the massive 160-foot video board directly overhead. You can't see the full field but the stadium experience is unforgettable. Seated tickets start at $200+, so the Party Pass saves 50%+ while still getting you inside.

Everything's Bigger in Texas

Including the value - if you know where to look. Skip the Cowboys premium, embrace Stars hockey, ride the DART, and discover why DFW's sports scene is deeper than America's Team.
