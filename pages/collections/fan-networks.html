Safety & Verification

Trust But Verify

Meeting strangers from the internet requires caution. Most fan community members are genuine, but scammers exist. The strategies below have protected thousands of fans from bad situations. Don't skip these steps.

Vetting Travel Partners

Social Media Check

Real fans have posting history. Check their Reddit/Facebook/Twitter for sports content. Years of genuine fandom is hard to fake. No history = red flag.

Look for: 1+ year history

Video Call First

Before committing to travel, do a video call. 10 minutes of face-to-face conversation reveals a lot. Anyone refusing this step isn't worth the risk.

Duration: 10-15 minutes

Start with Groups

Your first meetup should be at a public watch party or group tailgate. See how they interact with others before one-on-one travel.

First meetup: Always public

References Check

Ask who else they've traveled with. Real road warriors have references. Connect with previous travel partners to verify.

Request: 2+ references

Red Flags to Watch For

  • Pressure to pay via Friends & Family or Zelle
  • New accounts with no posting history
  • Refusing video calls or public meetups first
  • Prices significantly below market rate
  • Tickets still "with Ticketmaster" that can't be transferred yet
  • Vague answers about seats, sections, or travel logistics

Fan Networks FAQ

How do I find travel partners for a specific game?

Start with your team's subreddit - search for "[opponent city] travel" or post in the weekly thread 2-4 weeks before the game. Facebook groups like "[Team] Ticket Exchange" are also active. Be specific about dates, your situation (need hotel roommate, have extra ticket, looking for carpool), and respond quickly to interest.

Is it safe to travel with people I met online?

It can be, with proper vetting. Check their social media history (look for years of genuine fan activity), do a video call before committing, start with public group meetups before one-on-one travel, and ask for references from others they've traveled with. Most fan community members are trustworthy, but scammers exist - never skip the verification steps.

What's the best payment method for splitting costs?

PayPal Goods & Services offers the best buyer protection for ticket purchases. For shared expenses like hotels, Splitwise tracks who owes what, then settle via PayPal or Venmo. For tickets specifically, request the transfer first before sending money. Never use Zelle or "Friends & Family" payments with people you haven't traveled with before.

How much can I realistically save using fan networks?

For away game trips, expect 40-60% savings on travel costs. A $300 hotel becomes $75-100 split 3-4 ways. A $50 parking pass becomes $12-17. Gas for a road trip drops 66-75% with a full car. Tickets can be 10-20% cheaper through fan networks vs. resale sites. A typical away game trip that costs $800 solo might be $400-500 with good travel partners.

Are official fan clubs worth the membership fee?

Usually yes, if you attend 2+ games per year. Most $25-50 memberships include presale access (which can mean $20-50 savings per ticket vs. general sale), away game travel packages at group rates, and exclusive events. Soccer supporter groups are especially valuable - organized bus travel, guaranteed away sections, and community built-in. The ROI is typically positive after one game.
