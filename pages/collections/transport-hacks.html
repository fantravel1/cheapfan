Rideshare Apps: Split the Cost

Long-Distance Ridesharing Platforms

Forget Uber and Lyft. Long-distance rideshare platforms connect drivers heading to games with passengers who split gas costs. Often 50-70% cheaper than buses, with door-to-door convenience.

Poparide

Popular in Canada and growing in the US. Drivers post trips, passengers book seats. Prices set by drivers but regulated by distance. Great for cross-border hockey trips.

Avg Cost: $0.08-0.12/mile Best: Canada-US routes

BlaBlaCar

The European giant now operates in the US. Massive user base means more ride options. Built-in profiles and ratings. Book directly through app.

Avg Cost: $0.10-0.15/mile Best: Major city pairs

Hitch

Specifically designed for intercity travel. Matches drivers and riders automatically. Fixed pricing based on route. Ideal for regular commuters and game-day travelers.

Avg Cost: $15-40/trip Best: East Coast corridor

Facebook Rideshare Groups

Search "[City] to [City] rideshare" on Facebook. Massive groups exist for major routes. No platform fees, direct negotiation. Verify profiles before booking.

Cost: Negotiable Risk: Verify carefully

Rideshare Safety Tips

  • Always check driver/passenger ratings and reviews
  • Share trip details with a friend or family member
  • Meet in public places for pickup
  • Use in-app payment when possible (creates paper trail)
  • Trust your instincts - cancel if something feels off

Overnight Bus Strategy: Sleep Your Way There

The Ultimate Budget Hack

Take an overnight bus, save on a hotel. You leave at 11 PM, sleep on the bus, arrive at 6-7 AM. Explore the city, catch the game, take an overnight bus home. Total transport + lodging cost: $40-80 instead of $200+.

The Math That Changed Everything

Traditional weekend trip: $60 gas + $40 parking + $120 hotel = $220 before tickets
Overnight bus strategy: $35 bus there + $35 bus back = $70 total
Savings: $150 per trip

Flight Deal Hacks: When Flying is Cheaper Than Driving

Yes, Flights Can Be Budget-Friendly

With the right strategy, flying to an away game can cost less than driving. The key: deal newsletters, error fares, and understanding airline pricing patterns. Master these and you'll fly for $50-100 round trip.

Deal Newsletter Must-Haves

Subscribe to flight deal newsletters that alert you to price drops. When a $200 fare drops to $49, you'll know within hours.

Top: Scott's Cheap Flights Also: Secret Flying, The Flight Deal

Error Fares

Airlines make pricing mistakes. When NYC-LA shows up for $89 round trip, that's an error fare. They usually honor them. Follow error fare trackers on Twitter/X.

Frequency: 3-5 per week nationally Window: Often 1-4 hours

Budget Carrier Alerts

Spirit, Frontier, Allegiant, and Southwest run flash sales. Sign up for their email lists. $29-49 one-way fares happen regularly.

Best Days: Tuesday, Wednesday Book: 3-6 weeks out

Nearby Airport Trick

Check alternate airports. Flying into Oakland instead of SFO, Burbank instead of LAX, or Providence instead of Boston often saves $50-150.

Savings: 20-40% typical Trade-off: Longer ground transit

Transport Hacks FAQ

Is taking a bus to a game really worth it?

For trips under 4 hours, absolutely. You save on gas, parking ($30-60 at most stadiums), and the stress of driving. Most modern buses have WiFi, outlets, and decent legroom. The money saved often covers your ticket cost. For longer trips, overnight buses double as free lodging.

How far in advance should I book bus tickets?

The sweet spot is 14-21 days before travel for the best prices. Megabus releases their cheapest fares ($1-5) exactly 60 days ahead. Same-day bookings typically cost 3-4x more. For major sporting events or holiday weekends, book 3-4 weeks out as buses fill up.

Are rideshare apps safe for long trips?

Platforms like Poparide and BlaBlaCar have verification systems and user ratings. Check driver reviews, share your trip details with someone, and meet in public places. Millions of people use these services safely. Use common sense, trust your instincts, and stick to established platforms with review systems.

How do I find error fares for flights?

Subscribe to deal newsletters like Scott's Cheap Flights and Secret Flying. Follow accounts on Twitter/X that track error fares. When you see one, book immediately - they typically last only 1-4 hours. Always book directly with the airline (not third parties) as airlines are more likely to honor direct bookings.

What if I miss my bus after a late game?

Always book buses that depart at least 2 hours after the game's expected end time (add 30-45 min for overtime possibility). Most bus stations are near downtown, so grab food and wait. If you miss your bus, Greyhound often allows changes for a fee, or check for later departures. Have a backup plan: note the last bus/train times and keep a rideshare app ready.
