Why Road Trip?

The Math That Changes Everything

Flying to one city for one game = expensive. But once you're in a car, marginal cost per additional city drops dramatically. Drive 5 hours, see 3 stadiums vs. fly home after one. Same gas, triple the experience. This is the budget fan's multiplier.

$85 avg. per-game cost on trips
4-6 optimal games per trip
$0.22 cost per mile (avg. car)
3-4 friends = cost divided

The Ultimate: Visit All 30 MLB Stadiums

The bucket list. One summer. 30 stadiums. It's been done for under $5,000.

30 MLB stadiums
35-45 days optimal
~10,000 miles of driving
$4,500 budget target (one person)

The Budget 30 Strategy

Route Planning

Start in Seattle or Miami. Work in geographic clusters. Don't backtrack. The optimal route minimizes driving while hitting teams when they're home.

Ticket Budget

Target $15-25 average per game. That's $450-750 for all 30. Tuesday/Wednesday weeknight games at budget teams bring the average down.

Sleep Cheap

Mix of: friends' couches, cheap motels ($50-70), car camping at Walmart lots (free). 30 nights at $60 average = $1,800. Can be done cheaper.

Food Strategy

Cooler in the car. Grocery stores. One splurge meal per city for local flavor. $25-30/day = $1,000-1,200 total.

Road Trip Planning Essentials

Schedule First

Check team schedules before booking anything. You need teams to be home when you arrive. Use ESPN or team sites to map out who plays where and when.

Book Tickets Last

Lock in hotels and transport first. Game tickets can be bought closer to game day (often cheaper). Don't buy tickets 3 months out.

Friends = Savings

Every additional person cuts per-person costs. Gas is the same. Hotel rooms can fit 2-4. Splitting costs is the biggest budget hack.

Flexibility Wins

If a ticket is $80 vs. $20 the next day, adjust your schedule. Weather looks bad? Swap days. The best road trips are the adaptable ones.
