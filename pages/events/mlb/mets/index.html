Best Value Games

01 Tier A

Weekday Games

Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday games see 30-40% lower prices than weekends. The casual fans stay home. The tourists plan around Saturday. Monday is often an off-day. Wednesday 7:10 PM starts are the sweet spot - late enough for after-work, cheap enough for your budget.

Price Range: $20-35 Best Day: Wednesday
02 Tier A

Early Season (April)

Opening week aside, April games are cold, uncertain, and cheap. The diehards show up. Everyone else waits for summer. Upper deck seats drop to $18-22. Bonus: the team is still full of hope, and so are you.

Price Range: $18-30 Risk: Weather delays possible
03 Tier A

Late Season Fade (September)

If the Mets are out of playoff contention by September, prices crater. Season ticket holders dump inventory. Hope fades, but tickets get cheap. We've tracked $15 upper deck seats during late-September Mets collapses. The baseball is still baseball.

Price Range: $15-25 Condition: Team out of race
04 Tier B

Day Games During Workweek

The Mets occasionally have 1:10 PM weekday starts. These are the cheapest tickets of the season. Most people have jobs. You have a "doctor's appointment." Upper deck runs $15-22.

Price Range: $15-22 Availability: Limited dates

Getting to Citi Field

01 Tier A

The 7 Train (Best Option)

The 7 train drops you directly at Mets-Willets Point station - literally at Citi Field's doorstep. $2.90 each way, $5.80 round trip. No traffic. No parking fees. No Uber surge. The train runs frequently on game days and you're surrounded by fellow fans. This is the New York way.

Cost: $5.80 round trip From Times Square: 35-40 minutes
02 Tier A

LIRR (Long Island Option)

The Long Island Rail Road stops at Mets-Willets Point from Penn Station. Faster than the 7 train (15 minutes vs. 35-40), but pricier at $8-12 each way depending on when you buy. Great option if you're coming from Long Island or want a quicker commute from Manhattan.

Cost: $8-12 each way From Penn Station: 15 minutes
03 Tier B

Driving & Parking

Official Citi Field parking runs $30-45 depending on lot proximity. Third-party lots on Roosevelt Avenue drop to $20-25. Post-game traffic on the Van Wyck and Grand Central Parkway is miserable. The 7 train is faster, cheaper, and less stressful. Drive only if you must.

Official Parking: $30-45 Third-Party Lots: $20-25
04 Tier C

Rideshare (Uber/Lyft)

Works fine getting there. Getting home? Post-game surge pricing hits $35-50 from Citi Field to Manhattan. The pickup zone is chaotic with 40,000 people trying to leave. Take the 7 train. Your wallet and your sanity will thank you.

To Stadium: $20-30 Post-Game Surge: $35-50

Food & Drink Hacks

The Flushing Chinatown Advantage

Citi Field sits at the edge of Flushing - home to one of the largest and most authentic Chinatowns in America. Walk 10 minutes from the 7 train station and you're in a food paradise. Hand-pulled noodles for $8. Soup dumplings for $10. Peking duck for $15. Eat before the game, skip the $18 stadium burger, and experience some of the best Asian food in New York City. This is the single biggest food hack in MLB.

01 Tier A

Flushing Chinatown Pre-Game

Get off at Main Street-Flushing (one stop before the stadium). Eat legendary soup dumplings at Nan Xiang, hand-pulled noodles at Lao Xi'an, or a full Cantonese meal at Asian Jewels. Walk to the stadium or hop back on the 7 for one stop. Stadium food can't compete.

Meal Cost: $8-15 vs. Stadium Food: Save $10-20
02 Tier A

Bring Your Own Food

Citi Field allows outside food in a soft-sided bag 16"x16"x8" or smaller. No glass, no cans, no alcohol. But sandwiches, snacks, peanuts, candy? All legal. Make a deli stop in Flushing for a $7 banh mi and walk in with it. Stadium security doesn't blink.

Your Cost: $5-10 Stadium Equivalent: $18-25
03 Tier B

Stadium Value Options

If you must eat inside: Nathan's hot dogs run $6.50 (cheap by stadium standards). The Shake Shack line is always insane - skip it. Box Frites does solid fries for $8. Water is $5.50 for a bottle. Bring an empty bottle and fill at water fountains.

Budget Meal: Hot dog + water = $12 Pro Tip: Empty bottle allowed
04 Tier B

Beer Strategy

Stadium beers run $14-16 for a large. The McFadden's bar area in center field sometimes has $10 beer specials. Better move: hit a Flushing bar before the game where beers are $5-7. One beer inside to get the experience, pre-game for the rest.

Stadium Beer: $14-16 Flushing Bar: $5-7

Mets Budget FAQ

What's the cheapest way to see the Mets at Citi Field?

Tuesday or Wednesday evening games against weak opponents (Marlins, Pirates, Rockies) in April or September. Upper deck seats drop to $15-22 on resale platforms like Gametime. Buy 90 minutes before first pitch for the best deals. A Wednesday 7:10 PM start vs. Miami in late September is reliably the cheapest Mets ticket of the season.

Should I go to the Subway Series?

Only if the rivalry matters more than your budget. Subway Series tickets average $130 - that's 189% above the season average. You can attend 4-5 regular Mets games for the price of one Subway Series game. The atmosphere is electric, but the value is terrible. Budget fans should skip it and use the savings for multiple games against other opponents.

How do I get to Citi Field on a budget?

The 7 train to Mets-Willets Point. $2.90 each way, $5.80 round trip. The station is directly at the stadium. Parking costs $30-45 officially, $20-25 in third-party lots, and post-game Uber surge hits $35-50. The 7 train saves you $25-45 every single game and is often faster than driving due to traffic on the Van Wyck.

What's the best food hack at Citi Field?

Flushing Chinatown. Get off the 7 train one stop early at Main Street-Flushing and eat legendary soup dumplings, hand-pulled noodles, or dim sum for $8-15. Then hop back on the 7 for one stop to the stadium. You'll eat better food for half the price of a stadium burger. This is the single best food hack in MLB - no other stadium sits next to a world-class Chinatown.

Can I bring food into Citi Field?

Yes. Citi Field allows outside food in a soft-sided bag 16"x16"x8" or smaller. No glass containers, no cans, no alcohol. But sandwiches, snacks, wrapped food - all permitted. Grab a banh mi or sandwich from Flushing for $7 and bring it in. You can also bring an empty water bottle and fill it at fountains inside.

Are Mets tickets really cheaper than Yankees tickets?

Yes, significantly. Mets tickets average $45 resale vs. $85 for Yankees - that's 47% cheaper. The budget floor at Citi Field is $15-18 compared to $30-35 at Yankee Stadium. Both are MLB baseball in New York City. The Yankees have the brand, the history, and the prices to match. The Mets have the value.
