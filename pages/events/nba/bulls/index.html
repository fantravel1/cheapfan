Getting to United Center

The UC sits on the Near West Side. Here's how to get there without overpaying.

CTA (Best Budget Option)

Take the Blue Line to Illinois Medical District station (5-minute walk) or the Green/Pink Line to California station (10-minute walk). $2.50 each way. The #20 Madison bus stops right at the arena.

Official Parking

United Center lots cost $30-50. If you must drive, book through SpotHero for nearby lots at $15-25. Street parking exists but fills fast and neighborhoods vary in safety after dark.

Rideshare from Loop

Uber/Lyft from downtown runs $12-18 each way. The designated pickup zone on Wood Street keeps post-game chaos manageable. Surge pricing hits hard right at final buzzer - wait 20 min or walk to Madison for cheaper pickup.

The Smart Play

CTA to the game, rideshare home. You save $15+ on parking, avoid pre-game traffic, and can splurge on a comfortable ride back when you're tired. Total transport budget: $20.

Winter Warning

Chicago wind is no joke. The walk from CTA stations feels twice as long in January. Dress in layers, bring hand warmers, and remember: you're saving $30+ on parking. That buys a lot of hot chocolate inside.

Bulls Budget FAQ

What's the absolute cheapest I can see the Bulls?

Our verified lowest was $32 for Bulls vs. Pistons on a Wednesday in November 2024. Realistically, budget $40-55 for the cheapest home games against rebuilding teams on weeknights. Upper 300s, but the sightlines are solid.

Is United Center's upper deck worth it?

Absolutely. The 300 level has steep seating that puts you closer to the action than many arenas' upper decks. Sections 310-314 and 329-333 offer center-court views. Avoid corner sections (301-303, 317-320) if you can - slightly obstructed angles.

When do Bulls tickets drop the most?

Two key windows: (1) 48-72 hours before game when season ticket holders realize they can't attend, and (2) 90-120 minutes before tip when sellers panic. For Bulls games, the second window is reliable because they rarely sell out weeknight games against non-marquee opponents.

Should I buy Bulls tickets in advance or wait?

For most Bulls games, waiting pays off. Premium matchups (Lakers, Warriors, Celtics, Bucks) might require advance purchase. But for 30+ home games per season, last-minute buying saves 25-40%. The rebuilding phase means plenty of inventory available.

Is public transit safe to United Center at night?

The CTA Blue Line and buses serving United Center are heavily used on game nights. Travel with the crowd, stay aware, and you'll be fine. The walk from Illinois Medical District station is well-lit with plenty of other fans. Post-game, the crowd moves as a group back to transit.

Bulls vs. Blackhawks: which is cheaper?

Bulls are generally 20-30% cheaper than Blackhawks for comparable seats. Hockey has fewer home games (41 vs 41, but hockey feels more scarce due to fanbase passion). For budget-conscious fans, Bulls offer better value per entertainment dollar in the same building.
