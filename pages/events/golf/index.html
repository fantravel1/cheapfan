The Golf Event Hierarchy

Professional golf pricing varies wildly by event prestige. Understanding the tiers is everything.

Tier 1: The Masters

$2,500 - $10,000+

The holy grail. Lottery-based ticket system with worse odds than most actual lotteries. Secondary market is brutal. Practice rounds slightly more accessible.

  • Practice rounds: $1,200-2,500
  • Competition rounds: $2,500-6,000
  • Sunday: $5,000-10,000+

Tier 2: Other Majors

$100 - $1,500

US Open, Open Championship, PGA Championship. Still prestigious but actually obtainable. Practice rounds are the play.

  • Practice rounds: $75-200
  • Thursday/Friday: $150-400
  • Weekend: $300-1,500

Tier 3: Signature PGA Events

$75 - $400

Players Championship, Arnold Palmer Invitational, Genesis, etc. Best fields outside majors. Actually affordable.

  • Early week: $50-100
  • Weekend: $100-300

Tier 4: Regular PGA Tour

$35 - $150

45+ events per year across the country. Same players you see at majors, fraction of the price. The sweet spot.

  • Practice/Pro-Am: Often FREE
  • Thursday/Friday: $40-75
  • Weekend: $75-150

Tier 5: Korn Ferry & Qualifiers

$10 - $40

Tomorrow's stars playing for survival today. Monday qualifiers are often free. Intimate atmosphere, future major winners.

10 Golf Budget Hacks

01 Tier A

Practice Rounds & Pro-Ams

Tuesday/Wednesday at most PGA events are dramatically cheaper or free. Pro-Am days let you see every player in relaxed settings. They sign autographs, interact with crowds.

Typical savings vs weekend 50-70%
02 Tier A

Monday Qualifiers (FREE)

Every PGA Tour event has a Monday qualifier where pros compete for 4 spots in the field. Free admission, tiny crowds, same-quality golf. You'll see future Tour winners grinding for their careers.

Cost $0
03 Tier A

Korn Ferry Tour

The PGA's development tour features players who will be on Tour next year. Scottie Scheffler, Collin Morikawa, Xander Schauffele all played Korn Ferry. Tickets: $20-40.

Avg ticket $30
04 Tier B

Military & Volunteer Discounts

Most PGA events offer free or discounted admission for active military and veterans. Volunteering gets you free access plus inside-the-ropes experiences (but requires multi-day commitment).

Military discount Often FREE
05 Tier B

US Open Practice Days

Monday-Wednesday at US Open sites are $75-150 vs $250-600 for competition. You see the same course, same players, and can actually move around the grounds without massive crowds.

Practice vs competition 70% savings
06 Tier A

Fall Swing Events

PGA Tour events after the Tour Championship (Sept-Jan) have reduced fields and lower prices. Sanderson Farms, Shriners, RSM Classic - great golf, $40-80 weekend tickets.

Fall event avg $50
07 Tier B

Champions Tour

Legends like Phil Mickelson, Bernhard Langer, and Ernie Els competing at relaxed venues. Tickets typically $25-50. Players are more interactive than on main tour.

Avg ticket $35
08 Tier C

LIV Golf Events

Controversial but accessible. LIV events price aggressively to fill stands - $75-150 for weekend tickets to see major winners. The atmosphere is different but the golf is real.

LIV weekend $100 avg
09 Tier A

Thursday Morning

First round Thursdays before 10am have smallest crowds. Top players often have early tee times. You can follow a group without fighting for position.

Crowd reduction 60-70%
10 Tier B

Open Championship Value

The British Open is actually the cheapest major. Practice rounds £30-50, competition £85-175. Plus you're in Scotland/England - make it a trip.

Avg vs US majors 40% cheaper

What to Know Before You Go

Golf Tournament Essentials

  • No phones on course: Most events require phones silenced or off. Some ban them entirely from the course (Masters).
  • Dress code: Business casual is safe. No jeans at some venues. Check event-specific rules.
  • Comfortable shoes: You'll walk 5-10 miles. Golf courses are hilly. Sneakers, not sandals.
  • Sun protection: You're outdoors for 6-8 hours. Hat, sunscreen, sunglasses.
  • Bring cash: Some concessions still cash-only. Prices are usually reasonable.
  • Chair rules: Many events ban chairs or restrict where you can place them. Check policies.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the cheapest way to see PGA Tour golf?

Monday qualifiers are free and feature players competing for Tour spots. Practice rounds (Tuesday-Wednesday) at most events are free or discounted. Fall Swing events (September-January) have smaller fields and $40-80 weekend tickets. Korn Ferry Tour events average $30 for future stars.

Is the Masters impossible to attend on a budget?

Nearly. The lottery odds are roughly 1 in 100 for practice rounds, worse for competition. Secondary market starts at $1,500 for practice rounds, $2,500+ for Thursday. If budget matters, consider other majors - US Open practice rounds are $75-150, Open Championship practice is $40-65.

Which major championship is most accessible?

The Open Championship (British Open) is the most affordable major. Practice rounds run £30-50 ($40-65), competition days £85-175 ($110-220). Tickets sell through public sale without lottery. Plus you can combine with a UK trip - the per-experience cost becomes very reasonable.

What is the Korn Ferry Tour and why should I care?

The Korn Ferry Tour is the PGA's development circuit - players competing to earn their Tour cards. Scottie Scheffler, Collin Morikawa, Justin Thomas, and most current stars came through Korn Ferry. Tickets average $30, crowds are minimal, and you're watching future major winners. It's arguably the best golf value in America.

Golf's Not Just for the Country Club Set

From free Monday qualifiers to fall swing events, professional golf is more accessible than its reputation suggests. Skip the prestige, follow the game.
