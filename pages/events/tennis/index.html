The First Week Secret

Week 1 (especially days 1-4) is the best value in tennis. All 128 players are competing. You can see Djokovic, Sinner, Gauff, Swiatek on outer courts for $60. By the second week, the field narrows and prices explode. More tennis, less money = week one.

The Grandstand Strategy

Grandstand Court (3rd largest) often hosts top-20 players in early rounds. Included with grounds pass. Better sightlines than upper-deck Ashe. This is where smart fans go.

Cost: Included in grounds Quality: Top-tier
04 Tier B

The Monday Start

Opening Monday has full draw (128 players each) and tons of inventory. Prices are lowest here. By Wednesday, upsets create demand for remaining stars.

Best Day: Opening Monday Savings: 25-40% vs. Wed

The Rain Check

Bad weather forecast = prices drop. Ashe has a roof now, but sellers still panic. Check weather 48 hours out and buy when rain threatens.

Savings: 20-35% Risk: Weather might be fine
06 Tier B

The week BEFORE the main draw, qualifying matches are FREE. You see players fighting for their spot. Future stars, unknown warriors, all for $0.

Cost: FREE When: Week before main draw

Tennis Budget FAQ

What's the cheapest way to see top players at the US Open?

Days 1-4 grounds pass ($35-60). Top players often play early-round matches on outer courts (Grandstand, Court 17, etc.) before earning their way to Arthur Ashe. You can see Djokovic, Alcaraz, Gauff on smaller courts for the price of a ground pass.

Is it worth going to the US Open without an Ashe ticket?

Absolutely. Many die-hard tennis fans prefer grounds passes. You watch 10+ matches across intimate courts, see future stars, and experience the festival atmosphere. Ashe is a massive stadium where you're far from the action. Outer courts are often better tennis viewing.

Are qualifying rounds really free?

Yes. The week before the main draw, qualifying rounds are free admission at most major tournaments including the US Open. You'll see players fighting for spots in the main draw - often young talents or veterans trying to extend careers. Pure competitive tennis, no ticket required.
