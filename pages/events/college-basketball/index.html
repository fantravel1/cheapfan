Understanding the Tournament Structure

March Madness pricing follows a clear pattern. Knowing the rounds helps you find the value.

First Four (Dayton)

$25 - $75

Tuesday/Wednesday before the main bracket. Four play-in games in Dayton, Ohio. Same tournament, minimal crowds, maximum affordability.

First Round (Thu/Fri)

$35 - $125

8 sites, 4 games per session. Two sessions per day. Best value in the tournament - you see 4 games per ticket. Upsets happen here.

Second Round (Sat/Sun)

$50 - $175

Same 8 sites, 2 games per session. Prices bump slightly but still reasonable. Round of 32 games have survived the first cut.

Sweet 16 / Elite Eight

$100 - $400

Regional sites - only 4 locations. 2 games per session. You're deep in the tournament now. Regional finals decide Final Four spots.

Final Four

$300 - $3,000+

One city, one weekend, four teams. Stadium setting. This is the bucket list experience. Plan a year in advance.

The Smaller Market Play

NCAA rotates through 8 first/second round sites annually. When it lands in mid-tier markets like Spokane, Albany, Des Moines, or Greensboro, demand drops significantly. Same tournament games, 40-60% lower prices. Check the bracket announcement in March and target the least sexy location.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the cheapest way to attend March Madness?

The First Four in Dayton offers tickets from $25-50 for NCAA Tournament games. First-round sessions (Thursday/Friday) at smaller host cities like Spokane or Albany run $35-55 for 4-game sessions. Thursday afternoon sessions are consistently cheaper than evening due to work schedules.

How do I get Final Four tickets at face value?

The NCAA runs an annual ticket lottery for Final Four at face value ($175-350 vs $800-3000 secondary market). Applications typically open in fall for the following April's Final Four. Apply every year - odds aren't great but it's the only way to face value. Some fans report winning every 8-12 years on average.

Are first-round tournament tickets worth it?

Absolutely - arguably the best value in the tournament. One ticket gets you 4 complete games in the first round (8+ hours of basketball). At $35-75 per session, you're paying $9-19 per game. You'll witness upsets, see future NBA players, and experience tournament atmosphere without Final Four pricing.

Should I buy tournament tickets before the bracket is announced?

For regional sites (Sweet 16/Elite 8), yes. Face value pre-bracket tickets are often 50-70% cheaper than post-bracket secondary market prices. You won't know which teams you'll see, but you're guaranteed 4 high-stakes tournament games. For first-round sites, prices don't spike as dramatically, so waiting is lower risk.
