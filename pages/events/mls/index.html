The Messi Tax

Inter Miami with Messi is the exception. Home games now average $95-150. Away games in other cities spike 200-400% when Miami visits. If Messi is your target, budget $80-120 minimum. Worth it? Depends on how much you love goats (the GOAT kind).

The Supporter Section Advantage

The Best Deal in American Sports

Every MLS stadium has a designated supporter section behind one goal. These sections are general admission, standing, and the cheapest tickets in the house. Also: the loudest, most fun, most authentic soccer experience. Drums, smoke, chants - this is where the energy lives.

Seattle vs. Portland? LAFC vs. LA Galaxy? NYC vs. Red Bulls? Prices spike 100-200%. Pick any other game for the same experience at half the cost.

USL Championship and USL League One are MLS feeder leagues. Same sport, similar quality, $10-20 tickets. Many markets have USL teams worth supporting.

USL Avg: $15 Quality: High

Most MLS teams offer family packages (4 tickets + food/drinks) for $80-120 total. That's $20-30/person including concessions. Check team sites directly.

Value: Excellent Best for: Families

When Inter Miami visits, prices at host stadiums spike 200-400%. If you want to see your local team, pick a non-Miami match. Messi tax is real.

Best Budget Atmospheres

Seattle Sounders - Lumen Field

67,000-seat stadium that actually fills for big games. The Emerald City Supporters are legendary. $25-35 supporter section tickets for one of the best atmospheres in American soccer.

Portland Timbers - Providence Park

25,000 fans in downtown Portland. The Timbers Army is the gold standard of MLS supporter culture. Standing, singing, smoke. $28-38 for the North End.

Atlanta United - Mercedes-Benz Stadium

70,000+ for big matches in a world-class venue. Supporter section is $25-40. The tifo displays are NFL-level production with MLS ticket prices.

Cincinnati FC - TQL Stadium

Newest stadium in MLS (2021), intimate 26,000 capacity, $22-35 for supporter section. "The Bailey" end is one of the loudest in the league.

MLS Budget FAQ

What's the cheapest MLS team to see?

Chicago Fire, Colorado Rapids, and San Jose Earthquakes consistently have the cheapest tickets - often $12-18 for supporter section. Houston Dynamo and New England Revolution are also excellent budget options.

How do I get tickets to see Messi?

Inter Miami home games are expensive ($95-200). When Miami travels, host stadium prices spike 200-400%. Your best budget option: Inter Miami home games in early season or midweek - still expensive but more available. Check resale 2-3 days before game for panic drops.

Is the supporter section intimidating for newcomers?

Not at all. MLS supporter groups are welcoming to new fans. You'll stand for 90 minutes, learn some chants, and get swept up in the energy. First-timers are obvious and people will help you. Just participate - nobody expects perfection.

Is MLS quality actually good now?

Yes. MLS now has Messi, Lorenzo Insigne, Xherdan Shaqiri, and dozens of players from top European leagues. It's not Premier League level, but it's entertaining, competitive soccer with world-class moments. And the atmospheres at Seattle, Portland, Atlanta, and others rival anything in Europe.
