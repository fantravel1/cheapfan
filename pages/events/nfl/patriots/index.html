Getting to Foxborough: The Hard Truth

Gillette Stadium has the worst public transit access in the NFL. Plan accordingly.

The No-Transit Reality

Foxborough is 28 miles southwest of Boston with zero rail service and minimal bus options. The MBTA commuter rail runs special game-day trains, but they're unreliable, overcrowded, and take 90+ minutes from South Station. Most fans drive or rideshare.

Gillette Stadium Food Hacks

01

Tailgate Hard

Eat and drink your fill before entering. Gillette lots open 4 hours before kickoff. Bring a cooler, grill, and everything you need. Enter the stadium fed and happy.

02

Clear Bag = Snacks

Gillette allows one clear bag (12"x6"x12") per person. You can bring sealed, unopened snacks. Granola bars, crackers, and candy are fair game.

03

Water Bottle Trick

Empty plastic bottles (up to 20oz) are allowed. Fill at water fountains inside. Saves $6 per bottle, and Gillette has refill stations throughout.

04

Skip Premium Sections

The Putnam Club and lower bowl have "craft" food options at 30% higher prices. Upper deck concessions serve the same brands for standard (still expensive) prices.

Best Value Food Inside

  • Kayem Hot Dogs: $8 - Local brand, decent quality
  • Dunkin' Coffee: $5 - It's New England, Dunkin' is everywhere
  • Soft Pretzel: $7 - Big, salty, pairs with mustard

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest way to see the Patriots play?

Training camp at Gillette Stadium is free and open to the public in late July through mid-August. For regular season, target December games against NFC opponents - we've verified tickets as low as $55 for upper deck seats. Preseason games average $30-35 with the full Gillette experience.

How do I get to Gillette Stadium without a car?

The MBTA runs special commuter rail service from South Station on game days ($20 round trip), but it's crowded and takes 90+ minutes. Better option: split an Uber XL with 3-4 people from Boston ($20-30/person each way) or Providence ($15-25/person). Providence is actually closer to Foxborough than Boston.

How much has Patriots ticket prices dropped since Tom Brady left?

Average resale prices have dropped 71% from the 2018 peak ($325) to 2024 ($95). This is the most affordable Patriots tickets have been since the early 2000s before the dynasty began. Even premium games like Bills matchups are significantly cheaper than the Brady era.

Is tailgating at Gillette Stadium worth it?

Absolutely. Gillette lots open 4 hours before kickoff and the tailgate culture is strong. It's also the best way to save money - eat and drink before entering the stadium where beer is $14 and hot dogs are $8. Bring a cooler, grill, and supplies. Many fans spend more time tailgating than watching the game.

Are upper deck seats at Gillette Stadium worth it?

Yes, especially in the post-dynasty pricing era. Upper deck sections 300-339 offer full field views at $55-90, saving $100+ over lower bowl. The main trade-off is weather exposure - in December, consider Club level (Section 200s) which has indoor concourse access for warming up, often available for $90-120 on resale.

What can I bring into Gillette Stadium?

Gillette allows one clear plastic bag (12"x6"x12") per person, which can contain sealed snacks and an empty plastic bottle (up to 20oz) to fill at water fountains inside. No outside alcohol, no backpacks, no large bags. The clear bag snack hack can save you $20+ on stadium food.
