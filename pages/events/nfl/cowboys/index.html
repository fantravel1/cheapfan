‚ùì Cowboys Budget FAQ

What's the cheapest way to see the Cowboys play?

See them on the road. Cowboys away games average $89 vs. $225 at home. The cheapest options are typically at Cleveland, Carolina, Jacksonville, and other budget-friendly stadiums. You'll save $100-150 on tickets alone, plus parking is cheaper and the experience of being a visiting fan is unique.

Are Cowboys standing room (Party Pass) tickets worth it?

Yes, absolutely. AT&T Stadium's Party Pass ($75-125) gives you access to standing areas with excellent views of the giant video board. Arrive early to claim a rail spot. The end zone standing areas are surprisingly good, and you have freedom to move around. Many fans prefer this to distant upper-deck seats.

Which Cowboys home game is cheapest?

Historically, Giants games are the cheapest Cowboys home games, with tickets sometimes dipping to $140-150. Panthers, Titans, and other non-rival, non-primetime opponents also trend cheaper. Avoid Eagles, Commanders, and any nationally televised games‚Äîthose carry 50-100% premiums.

How do I avoid expensive parking at AT&T Stadium?

Skip the $40-75 official lots. Park at Arlington Convention Center ($20, 15-min walk), check Craigslist/Facebook for local homeowners selling spots ($15-25), or use rideshare (often cheaper than parking for groups of 2+). The Six Flags lot sometimes offers free parking with shuttle service on non-park days.
