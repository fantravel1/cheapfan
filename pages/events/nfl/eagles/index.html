Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest way to see the Eagles play?

Training camp at the NovaCare Complex is free and open to the public most days in July-August. For in-stadium action, preseason games run $40-55 with full tailgate energy. For regular season, target NFC/AFC rotation opponents (Panthers, Cardinals, Jaguars) in December - we've verified tickets as low as $95 in the upper 200s corners.

How do I get to Lincoln Financial Field without paying for parking?

Take the Broad Street Line (BSL) subway directly to NRG Station at the stadium complex. A SEPTA fare is $2.50 each way ($5 round trip) versus $40-60 for stadium parking. Drive to a free or cheap lot along the BSL route, park there, and ride in. You'll save $45-55 and skip the post-game parking lot nightmare entirely.

Are Eagles fans really as intense as people say?

Yes, and they're proud of it. Eagles fans are the most passionate fanbase in the NFL by virtually any metric. They booed Santa Claus in 1968 and still bring it up. The stadium had a literal jail and courtroom until 2003. But here's the thing - if you show up in green and actually care, they'll adopt you immediately. The intensity goes both ways. They're loyal beyond reason.

Which Eagles games should I avoid if I'm on a budget?

NFC East rivalry games are budget killers. Cowboys games average $385, Giants games $355, and even Commanders games run $295. These rarely drop below $250 regardless of timing or record. If your budget is under $150, stick to NFC/AFC rotation opponents and Thursday night games where prices average 21% less than Sunday kickoffs.
