The December Discount

December/January games in Green Bay mean temperatures below 20°F, sometimes below 0°F. Fair-weather fans (literally) stay home. If you're willing to freeze, December games against non-rivals are your best shot at sub-$130 tickets. Bundle up. Really bundle up.

The Jacksonville Takeover

When the Packers play in Jacksonville, EverBank Stadium becomes Lambeau South. 30-40% of the stadium is wearing green and gold. "GO PACK GO" chants drown out Jags fans. It's a home game with $60 tickets and Florida weather.

Lambeau Cold Weather Survival

The Layer System

Base layer (moisture-wicking). Insulating layer (fleece). Wind-blocking layer (jacket). Repeat for legs. Add: wool socks, insulated boots, hand/toe warmers, balaclava. This is serious.

The Tailgate Warm-Up

Tailgating at Lambeau is legendary. Fire pits, brats, beer. Use the tailgate to warm up before entering the frozen stadium. Locals will welcome you with food and drinks.

The Concourse Retreat

Lambeau's concourse is heated. If you're dying, duck inside for a quarter. Hot chocolate ($7) and hand warmers ($5) are available. No shame in retreating briefly.

Seat Location Strategy

North end zone gets afternoon sun in December. Avoid the shaded side if possible. East sideline = warmer. Check your ticket carefully before buying.

Packers Budget FAQ

What's the cheapest way to see the Packers at Lambeau?

December game against a non-divisional opponent (Jaguars, Titans, Panthers type). Cold weather drives prices down 35-45%. We've seen tickets as low as $95. Buy 2-5 days before the game when sellers get nervous about unsold inventory.

Is training camp really free?

Yes. Every July-August, Packers training camp at Ray Nitschke Field (right next to Lambeau) is open to the public for free. You watch full NFL practices, and players often sign autographs after. It's a genuine Lambeau experience without a game ticket.

How cold does it actually get at December games?

December games average 20-30°F but can drop to single digits or below zero. The 2008 NFC Championship was -4°F with -23°F wind chill. Layer seriously: base layer, fleece, wind-blocking jacket, wool socks, insulated boots, hand/toe warmers, and a balaclava. This is not optional.

Is it worth seeing the Packers away instead?

If budget is the priority, absolutely. Packers fans travel extremely well - games in Jacksonville, Tennessee, and Carolina often feel like home games with 30%+ cheese-heads. Tickets are $60-80 vs. $150+ at Lambeau. You miss the stadium experience but get the team for half the cost.
